FAQ

An examination management system is specifically designed to coordinate, oversee, and monitor all aspects of exam procedures within a university or college setting.

The primary goal of exam management software is to automate the assessment of students' subject knowledge. This platform is favored by universities and colleges for its ability to overcome various challenges and limitations associated with traditional pen-and-paper exams.

An exam management system helps educators easily create and manage exams, assessments, and quizzes. They can also view student information, track progress through detailed reports, set exam schedules, and analyze performance results effectively.

Students find exam management systems beneficial because they provide a structured and organized way to take exams online. They receive immediate feedback on their performance, can access study materials shared by teachers, and track their progress through detailed analytics and reports over time.

An exam management system ensures your exams are secure by using strong security measures like secure login credentials and encrypted data transmission. It controls who can access exam content and uses features such as randomizing question order, setting time limits, and anti-cheating tools to maintain exam integrity.
